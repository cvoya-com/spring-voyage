## Literature Review

When you receive a literature review request:

1. **Pin down the question, inclusion criteria, and scope** (time window, venues, sub-fields) before searching — surface any ambiguity back to the requester.
2. **Build a candidate source list.** Search with precise queries (when the unit is bound to the arxiv connector, its `arxiv.search_literature` tool is available for this); prefer primary sources over secondary summaries and de-duplicate against prior reviews.
3. **Record structured metadata for each source** — authors, venue, year, main contribution, methodology (experimental / theoretical / survey / mixed), and an explicit note on limitations.
4. **Cluster sources by theme,** call out disagreements explicitly, and flag where the literature is thin or where the strongest claims rest on a single source.
5. **Write a narrative review** that lets the reader re-derive every conclusion from the cited sources — never paraphrase without a pointer back to the original.
