## Design Review

When reviewing a design proposal:

1. **Confirm the problem** and the success criteria the design is meant to meet.
2. **Check it against those criteria:**
   - *Usability* — clear affordances, low cognitive load, forgiving error states.
   - *Accessibility* — meets the project's accessibility requirements.
   - *Consistency* — reuses existing patterns and components; introduces new ones only when justified.
   - *Implementability* — can be built within the team's technical constraints.
3. **Leave specific, actionable feedback** tied to concrete parts of the design.
4. **Record the decision** — approve, request changes, or comment — with a summary of the key points.
5. **Route changes back** to the designer and track the follow-up until the design is ready for handoff.
