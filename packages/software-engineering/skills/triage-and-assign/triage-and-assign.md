## Triage & Assignment

When a new work item reaches the team:

1. **Classify it.** Decide the type (feature, bug, refactor, documentation) and roughly size it — a quick fix, a focused change, or something large enough to break down first.
2. **Pin the goal.** Capture what "done" looks like and the behaviour or decision the work is meant to deliver. An item with no clear outcome is hard to assign well.
3. **Match it to a member.** Route by expertise against the surface the work touches: the tech lead for design, architecture, and breakdowns; the backend engineer for implementation; the QA engineer for test and coverage work. When the fit is unclear, hand it to the tech lead to break down.
4. **Hand it off with context.** Deliver a brief that names the goal, the relevant code or issue, and where the reply should go — the member should not have to reconstruct the ask.
5. **Split large items.** When an item spans several surfaces, break it into independently assignable pieces so work can run in parallel.
